ACME CORPORATION — EMPLOYEE POLICY HANDBOOK
Version 4.2 | Effective Date: January 1, 2025

================================================================================
SECTION 1: WORK HOURS AND ATTENDANCE
================================================================================

Standard working hours are 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, Monday through Friday. Employees
are expected to be at their workstations and available during core hours of
10:00 AM to 4:00 PM. Flexible start times between 8:00 AM and 10:00 AM are
permitted with manager approval.

Employees must notify their direct manager at least 30 minutes before their
scheduled start time if they will be absent or late. Unexcused absences exceeding
three days in a rolling 90-day period will trigger a formal performance review.

Remote work is permitted up to three days per week for employees who have
completed their 6-month probationary period and maintained a satisfactory
performance rating. Fully remote arrangements require VP-level approval.

SECTION 2: LEAVE POLICY
================================================================================

Annual Leave
Employees accrue 1.5 days of paid annual leave per month, totalling 18 days per
calendar year. Leave accrual begins from the first day of employment. Unused
leave may be carried over up to a maximum of 10 days into the next calendar year.
Any leave balance exceeding 10 days at year-end is forfeited.

Sick Leave
Employees are entitled to 12 days of paid sick leave per year. Sick leave does
not carry over to the following year. A medical certificate is required for any
sick leave exceeding two consecutive days.

Parental Leave
Primary caregivers are entitled to 16 weeks of fully paid parental leave.
Secondary caregivers are entitled to 4 weeks of fully paid parental leave.
Parental leave must be taken within 12 months of the child's birth or adoption.

Bereavement Leave
Employees may take up to 5 paid days for the death of an immediate family member
(spouse, child, parent, sibling). Up to 3 paid days are granted for the death of
an extended family member (grandparent, in-law, aunt, uncle).

SECTION 3: CODE OF CONDUCT
================================================================================

All employees are expected to treat colleagues, clients, and vendors with respect
and professionalism at all times. Harassment, discrimination, or bullying of any
kind — including on the basis of race, gender, age, religion, disability, or
sexual orientation — is strictly prohibited and may result in immediate termination.

Employees must not engage in any outside employment or business activity that
conflicts with the interests of Acme Corporation without prior written approval
from the HR department. This includes serving on the board of a competitor or
owning more than 5% equity in a competing business.

Confidential company information — including financial data, client lists,
product roadmaps, and proprietary processes — must not be disclosed to any
external party without explicit written authorization from the Legal department.
This obligation persists for 24 months after the end of employment.

SECTION 4: EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT
================================================================================

Business-related expenses are reimbursable when pre-approved by a manager and
submitted within 30 days of being incurred. Submissions older than 30 days will
not be processed.

Meal allowances: Up to $40 per person for client lunches; up to $75 per person
for client dinners. Team meals (internal only) are capped at $25 per person.
Alcohol may be expensed only for client entertainment and must not exceed 20% of
the total bill.

Travel: Economy class is the standard for all domestic flights. Business class
may be approved for international flights exceeding 8 hours. Hotel bookings must
not exceed the city rate cap published quarterly by the Finance team. Employees
are encouraged to book travel at least 14 days in advance to minimise costs.

All expenses must be submitted via the Concur expense management portal with
itemised receipts attached. Credit card statements alone are not accepted as
proof of expenditure.

SECTION 5: IT AND DATA SECURITY
================================================================================

Employees must not install unauthorised software on company-issued devices.
All software installations require a request through the IT helpdesk portal.
Requests are typically processed within 2 business days.

Passwords must be at least 14 characters long, include a mix of uppercase,
lowercase, numbers, and symbols, and must be changed every 90 days. Reuse of
the last 10 passwords is prohibited. Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is
mandatory for all company systems.

Company data must not be stored on personal devices or personal cloud accounts
(e.g. personal Google Drive, Dropbox). Approved cloud storage is limited to
the company's Microsoft 365 OneDrive environment.

Employees who suspect a security incident — including lost/stolen devices,
phishing attempts, or unauthorised access — must report it to the IT Security
team at security@acmecorp.com within 1 hour of discovery.

SECTION 6: PERFORMANCE REVIEWS AND PROMOTIONS
================================================================================

Formal performance reviews are conducted twice per year: in June and December.
Each review includes a self-assessment, peer feedback, and a manager evaluation
scored on a 5-point scale across five competencies: Delivery, Collaboration,
Communication, Innovation, and Leadership.

Employees who score an average of 4.0 or above are eligible for a merit-based
salary increase of up to 12%. Employees scoring below 2.5 are placed on a
90-day Performance Improvement Plan (PIP). Failure to meet PIP targets may
result in termination.

Promotions are not tied to the biannual review cycle and may occur at any time.
A promotion requires a recommendation from the direct manager, endorsement from
the department head, and final approval from HR. Employees must have been in
their current role for a minimum of 12 months to be eligible for promotion,
unless an exception is granted by the CHRO.

================================================================================
SECTION 7: GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE
================================================================================

Employees who have a workplace grievance should first attempt to resolve the
matter informally with the relevant party. If the matter is unresolved within
5 business days, the employee may file a formal grievance by submitting the
Grievance Form to HR.

HR will acknowledge receipt of the formal grievance within 2 business days and
will complete an investigation within 20 business days. Both parties will be
notified of the outcome in writing. Appeals must be submitted within 10 business
days of receiving the outcome.

Employees who raise a grievance in good faith are protected from retaliation.
Any retaliation is itself a disciplinary matter that may result in termination.
